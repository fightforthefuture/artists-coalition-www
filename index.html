We, the members of the Coalition, are cultural activists working across mediums and disciplines to protect freedom on the Internet. The Coalition was born out of the belief that when artists combine their vision and influence with the passions of the Internet activist community, we can together create a force for good that protects the best parts of the Internet now and into the future.

What we believe

  • Copyright laws are misaligned with the way that people are now creating content
    • The laws may have worked in the past, but we now live in a contemporary moment when the way we make art and communicate ideas has fundamentally changed. We need better laws that allow more people to participate as creators and makers and to share their work widely without killing creativity.
    • The copyright lobby have been expanding copyright's reach in order to "protect creativity". We believe that it is time to start over from scratch - with artists, young people, and users at the table to create copyright laws that work for us.
    • Stop the DMCA’s “assumed guilty until proven innocent” logic that can lead to inappropriate video takedowns on Youtube. Examples include how bands have to send separate video links to everyone in Germany because the rightsholders in Germany don't have a deal with Youtube.
  • Censorship is an attack to the human spirit.
    • Censoring people's inherent desire to create and communicate is the wrong way to approach things if you are trying to solve a problem. We want to return to the core values around intellectual property - ie a fertile space where people can share and build upon one another’s ideas.
  • Surveillance eats away at freedom of expression.
    • Artists need to be able to create without fear. This means fear of being sued (eg ‘Selma’ filmmakers having to rewrite MLK speeches); fear of having an art project targeted as an act of terrorism (eg the Steve Kurtz - Critical Art Ensemble - bio terrorism case) or fear of arbitrarily having your work or online presence get censored by over stringent copyright laws (eg DCMA “guilty til proven innocent” video takedown practices on Youtube, Music Label versus Dancing Baby case)
  • Culture means copying, seeing, learning, and building.
    • Copying is a set of cultural building blocks and opens up space for more freedom. An Internet that lets people copy (by linking, posting, modifying images, sounds, etc.) allows more people to participate.
  • Culture should be open and accessible to everyone.
    • Putting the bulk of cultural content behind paywalls is like destroying public institutions or the concept of education for all.
    • According to a study of Amazon book titles, 20th century copyright laws have cause whole generations of our literary and heritage to vanish.
  • Creativity benefits from collaboration.
    • Phenomenon of fans participating in a way that made a simple idea into a very big deal.
